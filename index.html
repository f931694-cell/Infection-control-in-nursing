Home & About

Welcome to our professional platform. Infection control is a cornerstone of modern nursing care, aiming to reduce microbial transmission and ensure the safety of both patients and healthcare providers.

"Adhering to infection control guidelines is not just a duty; it's a commitment to saving lives."

Key Guidelines

Hand Hygiene

  • Wash hands before and after patient contact.
  • Use alcohol-based rub for 20-30 seconds if hands aren't visibly soiled.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

  • Donning (Putting on): Gown → Mask → Goggles/Face Shield → Gloves.
  • Doffing (Taking off): Gloves → Goggles → Gown → Mask.

Sterilization

  • Follow hospital protocols for disinfecting medical equipment.
  • Ensure safe disposal of sharps and needles.

Common Healthcare-Associated Infections

  • Urinary Tract Infections (UTI).
  • Surgical Site Infections (SSI).
  • Respiratory and Bloodstream infections.

Nurses’ Compliance

Compliance refers to how strictly healthcare staff follow established protocols.

Improving Compliance:

  • Ongoing professional training and workshops.
  • Ensuring constant availability of PPE and sanitizers.
  • Regular monitoring and feedback from supervisors.
