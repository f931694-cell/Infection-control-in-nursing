Home Page

Welcome to the Infection Control in Nursing Platform. This website is designed to support nurses in preventing and controlling infections in healthcare settings to ensure patient and staff safety.

"Infection control is an essential part of nursing practice. Proper adherence to guidelines helps save lives and improve healthcare quality."

About Infection Control

Infection control refers to the policies and procedures used to reduce the spread of infections in hospitals and healthcare environments.

Importance:

  • Protects patients and healthcare workers
  • Reduces hospital-acquired infections (HAIs)
  • Improves quality of care

Guidelines

Hand Hygiene

  • Wash hands before and after patient contact
  • Use alcohol-based hand sanitizers

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

  • Use gloves, masks, and gowns when necessary
  • Follow standard precautions

Sterilization and Disinfection

  • Properly clean and disinfect medical equipment
  • Follow hospital protocols

Safe Injection Practices

  • Use sterile equipment
  • Never reuse needles

Common Infections

  • Urinary Tract Infections (UTI)
  • Surgical Site Infections (SSI)
  • Respiratory infections
  • Bloodstream infections

Nurses’ Compliance

Compliance refers to how well nurses follow infection control guidelines.

Factors affecting compliance:

  • Lack of training
  • Work overload
  • Limited resources

Ways to improve compliance:

  • Continuous education and training
  • Monitoring and evaluation
  • Availability of supplies

Self-Assessment

Ask yourself:

  • Do I perform hand hygiene regularly?
  • Do I use PPE correctly?
  • Do I follow infection control protocols?
