What is Infection Control?

Infection control is a practical, evidence-based approach which prevents patients and health workers from being harmed by avoidable infection. It is a fundamental part of nursing care and clinical excellence.

Standard Precautions

  • Hand Hygiene: The most important measure to prevent the spread of pathogens.
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Proper use of gloves, gowns, masks, and eye protection.
  • Respiratory Hygiene: Cough etiquette and source control.
  • Waste Management: Safe disposal of clinical and sharp waste.

Transmission-Based Precautions

Used for patients who are known or suspected to be infected with highly transmissible pathogens:

  • Contact Precautions (e.g., MRSA, VRE).
  • Droplet Precautions (e.g., Influenza, Pertussis).
  • Airborne Precautions (e.g., Tuberculosis, Measles).

PPE Donning & Doffing Sequence

Correctly putting on (donning) and taking off (doffing) PPE is critical to avoid self-contamination.

The Donning Sequence (Putting On):

  1. Perform Hand Hygiene.
  2. Put on the Gown.
  3. Put on the Mask or Respirator.
  4. Put on Goggles or Face Shield.
  5. Put on Gloves.

The Doffing Sequence (Taking Off):

  1. Remove Gloves.
  2. Remove Gown.
  3. Perform Hand Hygiene.
  4. Remove Goggles or Face Shield.
  5. Remove Mask.
  6. Perform Hand Hygiene again.
